Introduction

This is a simple pandemic model using a geometric serie. The population of sick people grows according to an initial growth rate. In addition, there are two ways of diagnosing sick people: a low fidelity test and a high fidelity test. Once a sick person is diagnosed, it is assumed to be put in quarantine, and cannot infect a new person. The performance of the tests are characterized by two parameters: compliance and success rate. The compliance corresponds to the proportion of sick people that can be tested. The success rate defines the proportion of tested people that can be diagnosed correctly. The value of those two parameters influences the effective growth rate of the pandemic. This example serves the purpose of illustrating that low fidelity testing can have a significant effect on controlling the growth rate.

Disclaimer

This is a toy model using a geometric serie, it is not based on any scientific evidence but purely intuition and I have no expertise in epidemic modeling.

Play with the model

For a given compliance and success rate of the low fidelity test, the plot illustrates whether the growth rate is controlled or not. The green region indicates a controlled growth rate (< 1) while the red region indicates an uncontrolled growth rate (> 1).

The slider allows to control the compliance of the high fidelity test whose success rate is 0.99. As the compliance of the high fidelity test increases we see that the region where the growth rate is controlled becomes larger. After roughly 20% of compliance, the whole design space is green, meaning that no low fidelity testing is required to control the spread of the pandemic. On the other hand, we can see that even with very little high fidelity testing, the growth rate can be controlled with a reasonable amount of low fidelity testing.
